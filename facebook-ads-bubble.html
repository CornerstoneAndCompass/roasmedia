Heard of the Facebook Ads Bubble?

Rich Burns | November 7, 2019

You've probably heard it by now: "Facebook ads are too expensive." "The platform is saturated." "Everyone is advertising there, so it's impossible to compete." "The cost per click has gone through the roof." These are the things people say when they're struggling with Facebook advertising—and they're half right, half wrong.

There is a Facebook ads bubble narrative that's been circulating for years. The story goes like this: Facebook ads used to be cheap and easy. Everyone and their cousin could throw $100 at Facebook ads and generate sales. But now that everyone knows about Facebook ads, competition is crushing CPM (cost per thousand impressions), and nobody can afford to run profitable campaigns anymore. The bubble is bursting.

But here's the thing: This narrative only applies to people who are running generic campaigns with mediocre creative to broad audiences. And they're usually running these campaigns incorrectly. If you're struggling with Facebook ads profitability, it's probably not because the platform is saturated—it's because your approach is.

The Saturation Myth

Facebook has 2.8 billion monthly active users. To call that saturated because some sectors are competitive is like saying the ocean is saturated because a few beaches are crowded. There's still vast opportunity if you know where to look and how to position yourself.

The real issue isn't saturation. It's that good advertising is hard, and it always has been. Mediocre ads have gotten more expensive. Genuinely great ads? They're profitable. That's not a platform problem—that's just how good performance marketing works.

The brands complaining about expensive Facebook ads are usually the ones running generic product ads to broad audiences. "Shop our collection!" isn't a compelling offer when your audience sees the same message from dozens of competitors. Of course that's expensive. You're relying on volume and luck rather than strategic differentiation.

What Actually Changed

The real shift in Facebook advertising isn't that the platform is more saturated. It's that measurement became harder and the competitive quality increased. When iOS privacy changes hit in 2021, brands lost some data about how their ads performed. Some people incorrectly interpreted this as "Facebook ads don't work anymore." What actually happened was "Facebook ads require better fundamentals now."

Simultaneously, the brands who figured out Facebook advertising years ago are now competitors with massive budgets and sophisticated strategies. The barrier to entry has risen. That doesn't mean there's no opportunity—it means you need to be strategic about it.

Brands that are winning on Facebook ads in 2019 and beyond share common characteristics: clear positioning, specific audience understanding, authentic storytelling, and relentless creative testing. These are the same things that made ads work in 2015. They're just no longer optional.

How to Stand Out in a Competitive Environment

If Facebook ads feel expensive to you, the first question to ask isn't "Is the platform dying?" It's "What am I doing differently from the brands that are profitable?"

Specificity in positioning: Generic brands running generic ads will always be expensive. Brands with clear positioning—"We're the eco-conscious athletic wear for busy professionals"—stand out and attract customers who value those specific qualities. These customers convert better and have higher lifetime value, which means profitable ads.

Understanding your actual customer: Most brands have a vague idea of their customer. Winning brands have detailed customer profiles: demographics, values, problems they're trying to solve, where they get information, what they fear, what they aspire to. This specificity allows for precision targeting and messaging that resonates.

Investing in creative quality: The difference between a cheaply-made product ad and a thoughtfully-produced brand story is massive in terms of engagement and cost. Brands that invest in professional creative—video, photography, copywriting—consistently see 2-3x lower cost per result than those using stock images and generic copy.

Testing relentlessly: The most profitable campaigns aren't lucky. They're the result of continuous testing: different angles, different audiences, different creative formats, different offers. The brands testing 5+ variations per week outperform those testing once a month.

The Difference Between Failure and Opportunity

Here's what's actually happening: Brands that got Facebook ads right in an easier environment are scaling happily. Brands that relied on shortcuts and Facebook's algorithm doing all the work are struggling. This is healthy market evolution, not platform death.

If you're new to Facebook ads or trying again after failure, the "bubble" narrative can feel disheartening. It's easy to think "This must be impossible." But what's actually happening is that excellence in advertising is more visible and more valuable. The gap between great campaigns and mediocre campaigns has widened. That's not bad—it's clarifying.

Brands that succeed on Facebook ads today do one thing consistently: they solve real problems for real customers with authentic, specific messaging. They test relentlessly to find what works. They invest in understanding their audience deeply. They treat advertising as a skill to develop, not a shortcut to revenue.

"The Facebook ads 'bubble' isn't about the platform being saturated. It's about low-quality campaigns becoming expensive while high-quality campaigns remain profitable. Which will yours be?"

The Opportunity is Real

If you can master Facebook advertising in 2019—through iOS changes, algorithm updates, and increased competition—you're building a skill that will be valuable indefinitely. The brands doing this today aren't worried about saturation because they're focused on excellence.

Is there a Facebook ads bubble? Sure, if you define it as generic, low-effort campaigns becoming more expensive. But for strategic, well-executed campaigns targeting specific audiences with compelling offers and authentic messaging? There's never been more opportunity. The platform has 2.8 billion people on it. The question isn't whether there's opportunity. The question is whether your ads are compelling enough to capture it.

Stop listening to the saturation narrative. Start focusing on making ads so good that they're not competing on price—they're competing on value. That's where profitable advertising lives.
